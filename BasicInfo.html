What is green energy?

Green energy, in simple terms, is energy that can be produced or retrieved from a source using a method that causes no harm to the environment. It is often associated with renewable energy, but there is a difference between the two. Renewable energy is naturally sustainable energy because it can be naturally renewed. Some examples of this are wind and solar power. Renewable energy, however, may result in carbon emissions during the process of generating the energy, which is what makes it different from green energy.

How do these methods work?

Solar Power

The most common green energy source used in homes is solar power. Solar photovoltaic (PV) systems use sunlight and turn it into electricity. Depending on how many solar panels are used, solar energy can power an entire household and all its needs.

Wind

Wind energy in a home involves installing small-scale wind turbines, usually on rooftops or in yards, to harness kinetic energy from the wind. As the turbine blades rotate, this mechanical energy is converted into electricity by a generator. The generated power can be used immediately, stored in batteries, or fed back into the grid, providing a sustainable and renewable energy source for residential use.

Geothermal

Geothermal energy in terms of heating works by drawing heat into or out of your home in order to heat it or cool it down, whichever is needed at a point in time. Geothermal works for producing electricity by using the heated solution it draws up to heat water to make steam and spinning turbines with it.
